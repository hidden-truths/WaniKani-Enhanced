Design notes

  1. 1
    Speaking mode, docked. The same navbar-docked bar as Minna/Self-Talk (toggle + mic picker + speed + you⟷ref balance). Per-line record controls render only while it’s on; the mic auto-releases on tab-leave.
  2. 2
    Per-line record. Records against one lyric line; takes are private per-user (own record-compare scope range, e.g. 80000–89999). Saving a take also marks today’s practice streak.
  3. 3
    Reference picker. Choose what you A/B against — generalises the engine’s existing “reference = any voice.” ⌥-click also cycles voices, as it does elsewhere.
  4. 4
    The YouTube caveat. An iframe’s audio can’t be decoded, so the YouTube slice is by-ear (hear → record → play back). The full overlay rig (dual waveform, ▶ both, level-matched) needs a decodable reference — TTS or an upload.
  5. 5
    Dual waveform + compare. ▶ you / reference / →you / both / loop, the waveform, speech-window alignment, and volume normalisation are the record-compare engine verbatim — nothing new to build.
